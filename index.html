Are technical skills no longer enough?

Have you reached a point in your career where your technical expertise isn’t enough to navigate the challenges you face?

Perhaps you’re now managing a team—people with emotions, motivations, and unique needs. Or maybe the stress of admin tasks, budgets, deadlines, and office politics is starting to pile up. The list of non-technical challenges can feel overwhelming.

If this resonates with you, coaching could provide the support you need.

Can coaching help you?

Coaching is a proven process designed to help individuals grow, improve their performance, and overcome challenges. It provides a dedicated space for you to reflect on the areas you want to work on, free from judgment or interruption.

Having someone truly listen to you—someone who is there solely to help you—is incredibly powerful. Through coaching, you’ll gain new perspectives, increase your self-awareness, and uncover solutions or actions you may not have considered.

Sometimes, simply raising awareness about a problem can make it feel smaller—or even make it disappear entirely.

What to expect from a coaching session

My coaching style is conversational and collaborative. I ask open-ended questions, listen actively, and echo back to ensure I’ve understood you correctly.

I often use the GROW model (Goal, Reality, Options, Will) as a framework:

  • Goal: What are you aiming to achieve?
  • Reality: What’s happening now?
  • Options: What actions could you take?
  • Will: What will you do next?

During our sessions, you’ll do most of the talking. My role is to help you uncover solutions within yourself. The process is about empowering you to take action and make meaningful progress.
